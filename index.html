Research

Broadly speaking, my current research interest is understanding the geometry of everything around us and of abstract mathematical structures. I believe computational imaging contributes more to the first part, while Lie theory contributes more to the second.

The imaging side of my current research projects focuses on single photon imaging, using the method of ultra-wideband probing, somewhat like signal processing. I feel this is a good way to understand the 3D geometry around us, through very raw input photon streams acquired using a single-photon avalanche diode (SPAD) device.

The mathematics side of my current research involves studying finite and infinite dimensional smooth structures (Lie groups and Lie algebra), and a more generalized case of Lie groupoids and algebroids.

How to connect the two? That's an exciting question, but I don't know the answer. I feel the two will and needs to be connected, to model and visualize the existing geometric structures in the world and even abstract geometric structures.

In general, I find the idea of computational imaging cool, and I am interested in providing concrete mathematical principles together with exciting geometric visualizations.
